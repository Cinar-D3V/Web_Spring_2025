What is email?

Email (electronic mail) is a digital messaging system that lets users send and receive messages over computer networks. It allows communication through text, multimedia, and attachments, making it a good tool for personal and professional use.

Email Protocals

  • SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol): The standard protocol for sending emails from a client to a server or between mail servers. It ensures the proper routing and delivery of outgoing emails.
  • IMAP (Internet Message Access Protocol):A protocol used for retrieving emails from a mail server while keeping them stored on the server, allowing synchronization across multiple devices.
  • POP3 (Post Office Protocol v3): A protocol for retrieving emails from a server, but unlike IMAP, it typically downloads and deletes messages from the server, limiting access to one device.
